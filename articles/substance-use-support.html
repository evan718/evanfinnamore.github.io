Understanding Substance Use and Addiction

If you or someone you know is struggling with substance use, please call the Drug and Alcohol Helpline at 1-800-565-8603 or contact emergency services.

The Science of Addiction

Addiction is a complex brain disorder affecting behavior, motivation, and the reward system. Understanding the neuroscience behind addiction helps explain why recovery can be challenging.

Key Components of Addiction:

  • Brain Chemistry Changes
  • Behavioral Patterns
  • Environmental Factors
  • Genetic Predisposition

Evidence-Based Treatment Approaches

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps identify and change thought patterns related to substance use.

"Recovery is not just about stopping substance use; it's about creating a new way of living." - Dr. Gabor Maté

Key CBT Strategies:

  • Identifying triggers
  • Developing coping mechanisms
  • Challenging negative thoughts
  • Building problem-solving skills

Motivational Interviewing (MI)

MI helps individuals find personal motivation for change and resolve ambivalence about recovery.

Core Principles:

  • Express empathy
  • Develop discrepancy between goals and current behavior
  • Roll with resistance
  • Support self-efficacy

The Role of Support Systems

Building Recovery Capital

Recovery capital refers to the resources—social, physical, human, and cultural—that support sustained recovery.

  • Social Capital: Relationships and community support
  • Physical Capital: Housing, health, and basic needs
  • Human Capital: Skills and education
  • Cultural Capital: Values and identity

Harm Reduction Approaches

Principles of Harm Reduction:

  • Meet people where they are
  • Reduce negative consequences of use
  • Prioritize immediate safety
  • Facilitate access to treatment when ready

Recovery Pathways

Multiple Paths to Recovery

Recovery looks different for everyone. Common approaches include:

  • Abstinence-Based Programs: Complete cessation of substance use
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment: Combining medications with counseling
  • Harm Reduction: Focusing on reducing negative consequences
  • Integrated Treatment: Addressing co-occurring mental health conditions

Supporting Loved Ones

Guidelines for Family and Friends

  • Practice self-care and seek support for yourself
  • Set healthy boundaries while remaining supportive
  • Educate yourself about addiction and recovery
  • Encourage treatment while respecting autonomy
  • Consider joining support groups for families
