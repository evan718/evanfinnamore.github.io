Understanding Substance Use and Addiction

If you or someone you know is struggling with substance use and needs immediate help, please contact emergency services or call the Drug and Alcohol Helpline: 1-800-565-8603

The Science of Addiction

Addiction is a complex brain disorder that affects behavior, motivation, and the reward system. Understanding the neuroscience of addiction helps explain why recovery can be challenging and why professional support is often necessary.

Key Components of Addiction:

  • Brain Chemistry Changes
  • Behavioral Patterns
  • Environmental Factors
  • Genetic Predisposition

Evidence-Based Treatment Approaches

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps identify and change thought patterns and behaviors associated with substance use.

"Recovery is not just about stopping the substance use; it's about creating a new way of living." - Dr. Gabor Maté

Key CBT Strategies:

  • Identifying triggers and high-risk situations
  • Developing coping mechanisms
  • Challenging negative thought patterns
  • Building problem-solving skills

Motivational Interviewing (MI)

MI helps individuals find their own motivation for change and resolve ambivalence about recovery.

Core Principles:

  • Express empathy and avoid judgment
  • Develop discrepancy between goals and current behavior
  • Roll with resistance rather than oppose it
  • Support self-efficacy and confidence

The Role of Support Systems

Building Recovery Capital

Recovery capital refers to the internal and external resources that support sustained recovery:

  • Social Capital: Relationships and community support
  • Physical Capital: Housing, health, and basic needs
  • Human Capital: Skills, education, and mental health
  • Cultural Capital: Values, beliefs, and identity

Harm Reduction Approaches

Principles of Harm Reduction:

  • Meet people where they are
  • Reduce negative consequences of use
  • Prioritize immediate safety
  • Facilitate access to treatment when ready

Recovery Pathways

Multiple Paths to Recovery

Recovery looks different for everyone. Common approaches include:

  • Abstinence-Based Programs: Complete cessation of substance use
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment: Combining medications with counseling
  • Harm Reduction: Focus on reducing negative consequences
  • Integrated Treatment: Addressing co-occurring mental health conditions

Supporting Loved Ones

Guidelines for Family and Friends

  • Practice self-care and seek support for yourself
  • Set healthy boundaries while maintaining connection
  • Learn about addiction and recovery
  • Encourage treatment while respecting autonomy
  • Join support groups for families affected by addiction
