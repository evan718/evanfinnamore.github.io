Understanding Depression Through Different Perspectives

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) View

CBT focuses on how thoughts, feelings, behaviors, and physical sensations interact and maintain depression. Breaking this cycle at any point can help improve mood.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) Perspective

  • Present Moment Awareness: Learning to observe thoughts without getting caught up in them
  • Acceptance: Acknowledging feelings without trying to change them
  • Values: Identifying what matters most to you
  • Committed Action: Taking steps toward valued activities despite difficult feelings

Adlerian Therapy Understanding

  • Social Connection: The importance of community and belonging
  • Life Tasks: Work, friendship, and love
  • Goal-Oriented: Finding meaning and purpose
  • Courage: Building confidence through small successes

Practical Steps for Managing Depression

Morning Routine

  • Open curtains for natural light
  • 5-minute gentle stretching
  • Make your bed
  • Have a glass of water

Start with just one task - success builds momentum

Physical Well-being

  • Brief walks (even 5-10 minutes)
  • Regular sleep schedule
  • Balanced meals
  • Staying hydrated

Social Connection

  • Text one person daily
  • Join online support groups
  • Accept small social invitations
  • Share feelings with trusted friends

Activity Planning Example

Time Activity Difficulty (1-10) Achievement (1-10)
Morning Open curtains 2 4
Afternoon 5-min walk 5 7

Track your activities to see progress over time

Professional Support

  • Consider talking to your family doctor
  • Look for therapists who specialize in depression
  • Check if your workplace has an Employee Assistance Program
  • Contact local mental health organizations for resources
